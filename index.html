Approach to Engineering

There's rarely an all-inclusive guidebook to product development. Each product requires a unique blend of information from engineering textbooks, research papers and, most importantly, empirical testing. Helping you to avoid "Analysis Paralysis" and learn at an exponential rate, failure is your best friend in design. Fail early and fail often, to avoid failures when your product is in client hands.

Approach to Management

People produce their greatest work in a comfortable, communal environment. Gone are the days of relying on long hours and the threat of layoffs. Employees should have a sense of ownership over their work, ideally through profit sharing, and should feel comfortable to make bold choices, embracing failure and the lessons that come from it. Employees are humans, whose lives and families depend on reliable income. Management has a distinct responsibility to each employee, to ensure that income remains reliable against market fluctuations and investment decisions.
